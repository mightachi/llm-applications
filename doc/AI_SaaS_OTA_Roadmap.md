### WEEK 1 — Pick Your Niche and Build Your Hit List

**Daily time commitment: 3 hours/evening**

#### Day 1 (Monday) — Define your ICP
- [ ] Write down your Ideal Customer Profile in exact detail:
  - Hotel type: 2–3 star, independent (not OYO, not chains), 15–100 rooms
  - Location priority: Goa, Jaipur, Udaipur, Manali, Ooty, Coorg, Rishikesh (tourist towns = high pricing volatility = highest pain)
  - Revenue: ₹50L – ₹5 Cr annual (small enough to not have a revenue manager, large enough to pay ₹10,000/month)
  - Decision maker: Owner, GM, or Operations Head
  - Current state: Pricing manually or copying OYO/Booking.com blindly
- [ ] Write your "problem hypothesis" in 2 sentences. Example: "Independent hotels in India are losing 20–30% potential revenue because they price rooms manually, without access to the same real-time demand signals that large OTAs use. We give them the same intelligence at 1/100th the cost."

  *Opening (5 min):* "Thank you for your time. I'm not here to sell anything. I'm trying to understand how hotel pricing works in the real world. Can you walk me through how you decide your room rate for, say, this coming weekend?"

### WEEK 5 — Architecture and Data

**Daily split: 2 hrs outreach + 4 hrs build**

#### Day 29–30 — Tech stack setup
- [ ] Choose and set up your stack (all free to start):
  - Backend: Python + FastAPI
  - Database: Supabase (free tier, Postgres)
  - Frontend: Streamlit (fastest for MVP — no frontend experience needed)
  - Hosting: Railway.app (free tier for backend), Vercel (free for frontend)
  - LLM: Anthropic Claude API or OpenAI (for price explanation text generation)
  - Version control: GitHub private repo
- [ ] Install Cursor AI — this will make you code 3x faster. It's worth every rupee (₹1,600/month)
- [ ] Create project structure. Set up GitHub repo.
- [ ] **Target:** Dev environment running locally. Hello world on Railway.

#### Day 31–33 — Data sources research and setup
- [ ] Identify and test your 5 data sources:
  1. **Competitor rates** — Scrape Booking.com public rates for nearby hotels (use requests + BeautifulSoup, or a simple Playwright script). Test this for one hotel in Jaipur.
  2. **Local events** — Google Events search via SerpAPI ($50 free credits), or manual EventBrite API
  3. **Weather** — OpenWeatherMap API (free tier, 1,000 calls/day)
  4. **Public holidays** — Static list (just a JSON file with Indian public holidays + school holidays by state)
  5. **Hotel's own data** — CSV upload (occupancy history, past rates). Your design partners will give you this.
- [ ] Build a simple data ingestion pipeline: given a hotel name and city, fetch all 5 data signals into a Pandas dataframe
- [ ] **Target:** All 5 data sources returning data for at least 1 test hotel.

#### Day 34–35 — Pricing model v1
- [ ] Build your first pricing model (keep it simple — you can make it sophisticated later):
  - Base price = hotel's average rate from past 30 days
  - Adjustments: +15% if local event this weekend, +10% if competitor sold out, -10% if weather forecast is bad, +20% if holiday week, -8% if Monday/Tuesday (low demand days)
  - Output: recommended price for tonight + next 14 days
- [ ] This is NOT a neural network yet. A rule-based model is fine for v1. Your customers won't know the difference and it'll be explainable.
- [ ] **Target:** Given a hotel name, model outputs 14-day pricing calendar with adjustment reasons.

### WEEK 13–14 — Convert Design Partners to Paying

**This is the most important sales conversation you will ever have.**

#### Day 85–86 — Prepare your conversion pitch
- [ ] Calculate the ROI for each of your 3 design partners specifically. Use their actual data:
  - What is their current monthly revenue?
  - What is their average occupancy rate?
  - If occupancy improved by 5%, how much additional revenue is that?
  - How does that compare to your ₹6,000–10,000/month fee?
  - Example: 40-room hotel, ₹2,000 ADR, 65% occupancy = ₹1.56L/month revenue. 5% improvement = ₹7,800 more per month. Tool costs ₹8,000/month. ROI = near breakeven or positive from month 1.
- [ ] Prepare personalised 1-page ROI sheet for each design partner.

#### Day 87–91 — Conversion calls
- [ ] Book a call with each design partner. This is your first "sales call." Script:
  - Open: "You've been using the tool for X weeks. I want to understand what's been working and what hasn't."
  - Show their data: "Based on your usage, here's what the tool recommended vs what you actually charged. Here's the estimated revenue difference."
  - Transition: "I'm moving the product to a paid plan next week. For you, as an early partner who helped shape the product, I want to offer you the first 3 months at ₹5,000/month [instead of ₹8,000]. After that, it's the standard rate. Sound fair?"
  - Handle "let me think": "Of course. What specifically would help you feel more confident about this? Is it the pricing, the ROI, or something about the product?"
  - Handle "it's expensive": "I completely understand. Let me show you the number: at your hotel's scale, a 3% improvement in occupancy pays for 6 months of our subscription. Want to look at that together?"
- [ ] **Target:** At least 2 of 3 design partners convert to paid. This is Milestone 3 starting point.

## PRICING STRATEGY — COMPLETE GUIDE

### Your price tiers

| Plan | India Price | International | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | ₹6,000/month | $99/month | 1 property, up to 30 rooms, core pricing tool |
| Growth | ₹15,000/month | $249/month | 1 property unlimited rooms, all features, API sync |

| Enterprise | Custom (₹75,000+) | Custom ($999+) | Chains, PMS integration, dedicated support |

**Annual plan discount:** 2 months free (17% off) — push this hard for cash flow.

### The ROI conversation — memorise this

"A 40-room hotel in Jaipur at ₹2,500 ADR and 65% occupancy earns ₹2.37 crore per year.
A 5% improvement in RevPAR = ₹11.8 lakhs more per year.
We charge ₹72,000 per year on annual plan.
Your ROI is 16x. Still want to think about the price?"

### Objection handling

**"Too expensive"**
Say: "Compared to what? Let me ask — what does pricing incorrectly cost you per month right now? Last Diwali season, did you fill up before you could raise prices? That's revenue you can never get back."

**"We'll build it ourselves"**
Say: "Absolutely. Our in-house estimate for building what we have today is 9 months and ₹35–50 lakhs in engineering cost — and you'd need to maintain it. We can have your hotel live in 2 hours. But if you'd rather build, I completely respect that."

**"We need budget approval"**
Say: "Of course. To help you make the case internally — can I send you a 1-page ROI document with your hotel's specific numbers? It usually makes the conversation easier with your team."

**"We already use [competitor]"**
Say: "That's great — which one? [Listen.] Interesting. Are you happy with it? The main difference we see is [specific gap you know about that competitor]. Would it be worth a 30-minute comparison call to see if there's a meaningful difference for your use case?"

**"Send me a proposal"**
This often means: I want to end this call. Say: "Of course, I'll send one over. Before I do — if the proposal looks right on pricing and scope, is there anything else that might slow down the decision?" Surface the real objection now.

### Negotiation rules
- Always anchor high first. Start with Growth plan (₹15,000), then offer Starter (₹8,000) as a step down.
- Never discount more than 20% without getting something back: a case study, a 12-month commitment, a referral, or a testimonial.
- Annual deals: offer 2 months free instead of a percentage discount. Same value, but "2 months free" feels like a gift, not desperation.
- Free trials are 30 days maximum. After 30 days, charge. Do not extend unless they have a specific blocker you are actively solving.

## THE ONLY RULES THAT MATTER

1. **Never stop outreach.** Even the day you close your biggest deal, still send 10 emails.
2. **Talk to customers before building features.** If 2 customers didn't ask for it, it doesn't get built.
3. **Price on value, not cost.** Your tool costs ₹500/month to run. Charge ₹10,000.
4. **Follow up 5 times minimum.** Most deals close on follow-up 3–5. Giving up after 1 email is not sales.
5. **Churn kills faster than you can sell.** Spend 30 minutes every day making sure current customers are succeeding.
6. **Ship ugly, improve fast.** A working Streamlit dashboard sold to 3 customers beats a beautiful product nobody has seen.
7. **The first ₹1 lakh MRR is the hardest.** After that, the product sells itself if you've built something people love.
